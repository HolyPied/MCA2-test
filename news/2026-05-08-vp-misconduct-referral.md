The Executive Administration of the Minecraft Club of America has formally referred Vice President Kyden Blake to the World Court for judicial review and to the World Congress for any proceedings deemed appropriate under the Constitution, including possible impeachment consideration, following a preliminary internal review into alleged server misconduct.

## Background

Earlier this week, Vice President Blake made statements indicating that he had obtained access to restricted in-game materials — specifically smithing templates — through methods outside the intended gameplay structure of the server. According to statements made during subsequent internal questioning, these items were allegedly obtained through external FTP file access and the use of third-party applications.

## What the Review Found

Following these remarks, members of the Executive Administration initiated a preliminary internal review to determine whether a violation of MCA server law or executive conduct standards had occurred.

During the review, a sword named *Final Resort* was discovered in Vice President Blake's possession. The weapon contained level 255 enchantments — far beyond the limits of standard server configuration and unobtainable through legitimate gameplay. No other items with similarly impossible enchantment levels were identified during the preliminary review.

The Executive Administration also believes additional items may have been granted to Vice President Blake through unauthorized administrative means, though the exact quantity and scope remain unknown at this time.

## Allegations

Based on the evidence presently available, the Executive Administration believes there is reasonable cause to suspect:

- Unauthorized acquisition of restricted in-game items
- Abuse or misuse of administrative or executive-level access
- Conduct inconsistent with the responsibilities of a sitting executive officer
- Violation of established server integrity and fair-play standards

## Referral to Constitutional Authorities

In accordance with the MCA's constitutional structure, the matter has been formally referred to the World Court for investigation and judicial review, and to the World Congress for any proceedings deemed appropriate — up to and including impeachment should sufficient evidence be established.

[pullquote]This report is submitted not out of personal hostility, but out of obligation to the rule of law and the integrity of the institution itself. — National President William Rector[/pullquote]

National President Rector acknowledged Vice President Blake's record of service in his submission, stating that Blake's contributions to the administration and organization are acknowledged and respected. The Executive Administration has requested that all proceedings be conducted fairly, professionally, and with full respect for due process.

The investigation is ongoing.
